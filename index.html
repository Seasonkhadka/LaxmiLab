Pharmaceutical Efficacy
Analysis Laboratory

The Pharmaceutical Efficacy Analysis Laboratory explores the biological mechanisms underlying metabolic diseases, with a focus on insulin resistance, obesity, type 2 diabetes, and related complications such as neurodegeneration.

Our research examines how environmental pollutants—including particulate matter (PM₁₀), micro- and nanoplastics (MNPs), and persistent organic pollutants (POPs)—enter the body and disrupt metabolic homeostasis. We investigate their effects on barrier integrity, inflammatory signaling, transcriptional regulation, and insulin signaling pathways at the cellular and systemic levels. In parallel, we develop and evaluate evidence-based herbal and natural interventions aimed at restoring metabolic balance and protecting against pollutant-induced dysfunction. By integrating in vitro and in vivo models, our laboratory bridges environmental exposure science with therapeutic innovation to advance strategies for metabolic health.

Overview of Our Work

Metabolic disease research

We study insulin resistance, obesity, type 2 diabetes, and related complications including neurodegeneration and hepatic steatosis, using cellular and animal models.

Environmental exposures

We assess how particulate matter (PM₁₀), micro- and nanoplastics (MNPs), and persistent organic pollutants (POPs) affect metabolic homeostasis, barrier integrity, and insulin signaling.

Natural interventions

We develop and evaluate herbal and marine-derived compounds (e.g. Gracilaria chorda, Caulerpa species) for improving glucose and lipid metabolism and insulin sensitivity.

Models & methods

We use 3T3-L1 adipocytes, L6 myotubes, pancreatic β-cells, zebrafish larvae, and C57BL/6J mice to bridge in vitro and in vivo findings toward therapeutic strategies.
